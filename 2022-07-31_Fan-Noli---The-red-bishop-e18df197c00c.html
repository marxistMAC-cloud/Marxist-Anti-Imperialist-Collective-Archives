Fan Noli — The red bishop

One of the most important figures in the history of the Albanian nation is without a single doubt Fan Noli, a Harvard educated politician, Prime Minister, Archbishop, writer, critic, musician, poet, orator, scholar, journalist revolutionary and most importantly a flaming Albanian nationalist who fought to protect and serve his Fatherland. One could write entire books about Noli’s life and accomplishments but we are going to analyze the June Revolution of 1924 he led and his actions after the revolution that granted him a place in the Hall of Heroes of Albania. In the wake of the assassination of the Albanian nationalist, deputy and Hero Avni Rustemi by the forces of Zogu, Noli and others blamed the
Government and quickly gathered in Vlora forming the “Revolutionary Committee” with it’s members being Qazim Koculi, Koleka, Koço Tasi, Luigj Gurakuqi, and Xhemal Beg Bushati, the Committee appointed 3 military leaders Bajram Curri, Rexhep Shala and Kaçem Qafëzeza. Afterwards it called for an uprising against the government with demands being the eradication of feudalism, the establishment of democracy, military and legislative administrative reform, liberation of the peasantry, tax cuts for the lower classes, protection of the national economy from foreign capital, the organization of education on a national basis, raising the prestige of the Albanian state in the outside world and friendly relations with all states. An army of 6.000 was gathered and started marching towards Tirana taking city after city with no resistance, on June 10th the forces entered Tirana victorious and Zog fled to Yugoslavia. The resistance was little to non-existent, no one from the general populace fought against the revolutionaries except Zog and his clique, the outcome were 26 people dead and 50 people injured which again confirms that the population in no way supported Zog and the government. Noli organized a government that aimed toachieve the goals mentioned above, and to be specific, Noli had a 20-point program which was:

1. The general disarmament of the people without exception.
2. The denunciation of the inciters of fratricide and their main agents
with expulsion and confiscation of their wealth or other punishments.
3. The reestablishment of tranquility, order and the Sovereignty of the law.
4. To exalt the authority of the State over any personal or extralegal
power.
5. To eliminate feudalism, free the people and establish democracy in
Albania.
6. Radical reforms in all departments, both civil and military.
7. To simplify the bureaucracy and cleanse the administration.
Besides ability and morality, the patriotism of workers will be taken
into consideration.
8. The security and rights of the employees and the determination of
their responsibilities shall be established by a special law.
9. To organize the communes so as to improve the conditions of
villages and villagers and to extend powers at the village level.
10. Balance the budget by radical economies.
11. Change the tax system in a manner favorable to the people.
12. Improve conditions for the farmers and their economic
independence.
13. Ease entry for foreign capital into the country while defending and
organizing the economic independence of the country.
14. Raise the credit and prestige of the State in the outside world.
15. True independence of the judiciary.
16. Radical reform of the outdated tribunals.
17. To construct new roads and bridges and to take special care of the
means of communication in the country.18. Organization of the Department of Health to combat the diseases
that are ravaging the people.
19. Organization of the Department of Education on a modern and
practical basis so that the schools should produce capable citizens,
patriotic and able workers.
20. Friendly relations with all states, especially neighboring
countries.

The Noli government was very ambitious if we take into account the
situation of Albania at the time, the plan was to confiscate the
property of wealthy landowners so it can be redistributed into 4–6
hectares for a family up to 10 members and 8 hectares for a family
with more than 10 members. The governments of other nations saw
the Revolution negatively as it obviously threatened their ambitions
regarding Albania, first and foremost Britain refused to recognize the
government of Noli despite multiple attempts from the Albanian side
for a recognition. The British saw their plans for having the
monopoly over Albanian oil shattered, the previous government of
Ilias Vrioni promised a 200,000 hectare concession to APOC
(Anglo-Persian Oil Company) after the discovery of rich oil reserves
in Southern Albania around the area of Vlora, the government of Noli
with Minister of Finances Gurakuqi assessed that the terms of the
agreement weren’t favorable for Albania, specifically he addressed
the Shkodran newspaper “Ora e Maleve” where he stated that
Albania must retain control over her vital oil reserves since oil was of
“great importance for the economic life of Albania.” Also he
suggested that the refinement of the oil should take place on
Albanian soil in order to aid in the creation of other industries in the
country. It is worth mentioning that it was Gurakuqi that led the
successful attempts of the opposition in the National Assembly in
1923 before the revolution to block the ratifying of the agreementwith APOC. It seems that the French agreed with Gurakuqi as they
themselves were displeased with the agreement, characteristically
on 7 February 1924, the French delegation wrote to the Albanian
Foreign Ministry on behalf of Crédit Général des Pétroles, informing
it that the French government was unhappy with the intentions to
give the concession to APOC. They claimed that awarding the
concession to APOC would create a monopoly and violate the “open
door” principle. The Italians with their Compagnia Italiana di Petroli
wanted the concession to be given to them too and they were
prepared to give to Albania a loan of 100 million liras with the
guarantee of Mussolini himself if their wish was to be fulfilled, and
the American companies Sinclair Oil and Standard oil put their bids
for the concession offering more favorable terms than the other
bidders. The government could not decide since accepting the offer
of the British would mean that Britain would have a complete
monopoly over Albanian oil but also that would increase the chances
of a recognition from Britain, accepting the Italian offer would on the
one hand give Albania a much needed loan and in the other push
Albania further into the Italian sphere of influence and accepting the
offers of the American companies with better terms while seemed
worthwhile it would mean that they would anger Britain and Italy
and the US at the time wasn’t involved with European matters as
much as Britain. The government assessed that the British could
potentially offer better protection in the case of an attack but they
also considered, especially Konstadin Boshnjaku, that it was the
British again that agreed on the 1915 Secret Treaty of London that
split Albania between Italy, Greece and Serbia. What is also worth
mentioning is the increasing interest of the Americans for the
Albanian oil, even before the revolution of 1924. In 1921 the
Department of State Near East Division advised the Assistant
Secretary of State F.M. Dearing to “wake up to the fact thatthere are some things in Albania worth going after” and based on the
reports of American missionary Charles Telford Erickson, in turn,
F.M. Dearing suggested to the Commerce Secretary Herbert Hoover,
due to reports of substantial oil riches in Albania, that it was
advisable to “inform the principal American oil companies, by means
of a confidential communication, regarding the general aspects of
the oil situation in Albania.” Consequently Hoover wrote to the
Secretary of State Charles Evans Hughes in April 1922 recommending
to consider the issue of the recognition of the Albania state and if
that wasn’t possible he offered an alternative, to be exact “if actual
recognition appears inadvisable, to the possibility of sending some
American government agent into the country who can give a show of
interest and support the claims of the Sinclair Oil Company.” Later
Maxwell Blake visited Albania and the de jure recognition from the
United States came in July 1922. Maxwell Blake representing the
interest of the American state and the American oil companies
supported Zogu, specifically Maxwell wrote about him: “one
outstanding personality in the country” and he declared that Zogu
had “spirit and courage, qualities which amount for his present
position as the popular hero of the day … He is fearless, honest,
uncompromising, and malevolent toward his enemies..”. And
regarding the opinion of American officials I believe that we ought to
mention the writings and reports of the American sent in Albania
since 1919, Joseph Emerson Haven. Haven believed that Albania was
ripe to be a perfect colony of the Italian state and he argued that this
was the plan of the Italians. The Americans lastly estimated that they
couldn’t compete with the British and Italians for the oil bids since
these two countries had more people in their payroll and huge
influence. But the oil wasn’t the main reason foreign powers showed
hostility towards the revolutionary government of Albania. The main
reason was that their plans for the borders in the Balkans were under a huge threat. The British diplomat in Albania Sir Harry Eyres
expressed profoundly this hostility repeatedly mentioning that Noli
“should be hanged”, and Noli also showed great affection to Eyres,
instructing his friends to “knock Mr. Eyres down for me when you
see him.”. But what crime did Noli commit in order to deserve to be
hanged according to Eyres and the Foreign Office? According to
them, firstly he usurped the power illegally and disrupted the
peaceful and lawful conditions that existed in Albania under Zog. Of
course one might ask how peaceful and lawful is a country where
deputies are killed, attempts on the life on the leader are made every
other day (Zog only had 55 assassination attempts on his life and 600
blood feuds) and people can take the government with 26 deaths, but
we leave it to the experts and dear allies of the Foreign Office
to determine that and of course the modern Zogu supporters! Albeit
the main issue was not the disruption of the stability inside Albania
but the stability outside of it in the Balkan region, but how was this
so-called “stability” built? This Balkan stability was built upon the
lands of the Albanian people, the Western powers (and specifically
the British) made sure to split the Albanian lands and give them to
Greece and Serbia. This “stability” Noli threatened, this is why these
powers feared an Albanian state that is governed from a nationalist
government, they wanted to maintain the stability on the backs of
the Albanians. This is confirmed by the Yugoslav reports which
stated that the irredentists in Kosovo gave their support to Noli, and
also from the conversation between Yugoslav Foreign Minister
Nincic and Britain’s ambassador to Rome, Sir Eyre Crowe in which
Nincic expressed the feeling that Noli didn’t “offer any prospect of
stability” with Crowe noting that the Foreign Office was in complete
agreement. During the Premiership of Noli diplomatic ties with the
Soviet Union were established, the Soviet commissar for Foreign
Affairs Chicherin responded to the proposal of the Albaniangovernment for establishing relations positively and specifically
wrote “In addressing our very best wishes to the new Albanian
government, I express the firm conviction that the friendship
between our peoples, developing and growing stronger, will bring
abundant fruits for the welfare of both”. The press of the Western
powers and their Albanian lackeys accused Noli of being a
“dangerous Bolshevik” and created the nickname “Red Bishop”, while
the government of Italy, England and Yugoslavia and the reaction
inside Albania, created such barriers for the Albanian Government to
exchange diplomatic representations that when the Soviet
commissioner Krakovetski arrived at Durres at December the 16th
that he was soon forced to leave while a representative of the
Albanian government compared the situation of Albania with that of
a man “who has had a knife put to his throat and on whom any
demands whatever can be made”. At the end of August of 1924, Noli
and Gurakuqi traveled to Geneva to take part in the discussions and
meetings of the League of Nations and try to secure loans and
strengthen diplomatic ties with other countries, and this absence
lasted 2 months and it proved to be fatal for the bourgeois
-democratic revolution of Albania. The reaction inside with the silent
help and approval of the Western countries would strengthen its
position within the state and the main enemy of Noli and the
revolution, Zogu, had time to prepare the counter- revolution and
sign a secret treaty with the Yugoslavs in order for them to support
him with Noli himself noting “I aroused the hatred of the landed
aristocracy, and, being unable to drive it out, I thereby lost my
support among the peasant masses. My colleagues in the
government and a large portion of the officers were either enemies
of or, at best, indifferent to the reforms, although initially they
expressed themselves in their favor… Mr. Eyres was successful in
convincing those around me that agrarian reforms are a dangerousBolshevik innovation.” Later on November 1924 the Italian consul in
Albania noted “Although Mr. Eyres, understood that Ahmed Zogu
also is an individual in whom little confidence can be placed, he
holds that under the conditions that have arisen, Ahmed Zogu is the
only person capable to some degree of subjecting the Albanians and
instilling fear in them.” Meanwhile, Zogu was preparing for the return
and the subversion of the Democratic government of Albania. Finally
on December 11th Zogu started marching from Yugoslavia with 4.000
men towards Albanian, amd out of this 4.000 men, only 500 were
Albanian, belonging in the clan of Zogu from Mati where his family
ruled, the others were Yugoslav forces provided by Prime Minister of
Serbia Nikola Pašić and a few troops and commanders of the White
Russian army under the General Pyotr Wrangel. Zogu and the
Yugoslav forces entered Tirana on December 24th and declared the
triumph of lawfulness in Albania and sentenced Noli and his cabinet
to death in absentia, at the time Noli reached Italy via a ship where
he was held for one month and then left for Vienna. Inside of Albania,
Zogu declared the state a presidential democracy in January 1925,
with Zog of course being the President. Later, Zog, in order to abide
to the secret treaty he signed with the Yugoslavs and to gain support
from Italy, appointed at his cabinet Ceno Beg Kryeziu, his brother in
law, Minister of Internal Affairs, and Myfit Bej Libohova, Minister of
Finances and deputy Minister of Foreign affairs, known respectively
as agents of the Yugoslavs and the Italians. Moreover, Zog abiding to
the secret treaty with the Yugoslavs had to give to Yugoslavia the
areas of Shën Naum and Vermosh, and in order to make it that
happen without outrage from the populace, ordered Mehdi Frashëri
to present this as an exchange of territories between the 2 states
were allegedly Albania would even profit from this by gaining
territories double in size! The reality was of course different, and the
areas that would supposedly be exchanged and given to the Albanian state had all already been secured by the 1913 Conference of
Ambassadors were the independence of Albania was first recognized,
and also the government tried to present this exchange of territories
as a demand from the League of Nations while the League never
made such demands, and on top of that, to completely avoid
responsibility, the law for the “exchange” of territories was voted and
ordered by the Senate and the Parliament towards the government
headed by Zogu, and now of course the fact that both Senate and
Parliament member where picked by the The President is completely
irrelevant! The Legislative Assembly on the August of 1925 presented
the following act: “The Senate and the Chamber of Deputies
gathered together today, July 18, 1925, to discuss the issue of the
Albanian-Yugoslav borders, accept in principle a referendum on an
exchange of land between the two states and authorize the
government to enter into appropriate agreements in accordance
with the interests of the state and the Albanian people”. The
government in turn presented the decision to the international stage
which was accepted and surprised many, and this act also raised
potential threats against the territorial sovereignty of Albania
because Greece claimed that this act sets a principle and they could
follow the same route as the Yugoslavs to take the 14 villages in the
Southern region around Bilisht given to Albania 1913. The “exchange”
was finalized one year later, in 1926 with the approval of the League
of Nations, the news of this “exchange” were received with great joy
in the Yugoslav Kingdom. Outside of the Albanian borders Fan Noli
founded and headed KONARE (National Revolutionary Committee)
where he worked with like-minded Albanian patriots who wanted to
overthrow the tyranny of Zogu. Fan Noli as the head of KONARE
would protest the ceding of the Albanian territories and would
develop close ties with the Communist world and the Soviet Union.
In November 1927, Noli, representing Albanian patriots of the timeand now the now renamed KONARE as the “National Liberation
Committee”, attended the Congress of Friends of the Soviet Union
where he declared “The success of the congress does not surprise
me at all, for it is entirely natural that all workers and all oppressed
peoples of the world, whose representatives are participating in the
congress, regard the USSR as their fatherland.” And “I am inspired by
the fact that I personally saw the first workers’ and peasants’ state,
which has a tremendous future and which is the precursor of similar
workers’ and peasants’ republics of the future”. By 1928 the
Committee was de facto part of the Comintern and 24 young
Albanian patriots would be sent to Moscow for studies at the
best Soviet Institutions. The Committee and Noli didn’t have success
in overthrowing the regime of Zogu or even posing a real threat, and
a disappointed Noli would retire from active political life and
immigrate to the United States. There, Noli devoted himself to
philosophy, religion, music and history, and he would compose,
complete his PHD, critique and translate literature and enrich the
Albanian culture and intellect in ways no one at the time did, but he
was also informed of the news in Albania and the world and with
great interest he saw the transformation and routes his former
comrades took, most of them become Communist intellectuals and
founded Communist groups inside and outside of Albania and later
become prominent Communist politicians. In 1939 before World War
2, Fascist Italy would invade Albania with Zogu leaving the country
and stealing the gold, which sparked the resistance of Albania,
carried out mainly by the Partisans against Italians and later
Germans. The Partisans headed by Hoxha, liberated the country
without the help of foreign soldiers and they entered Tirana
declaring a new era in Albania, the era of People’s power.
Noli saw this with great enthusiasm and supported in ever way thegovernment Hoxha. The propaganda machine of the Albanian state
today tries to hide this fact or minimize it by claiming that Noli had
forgiven and supported Zog and that towards the end of his life he
wasn’t close with Hoxha or that the State Security of Socialist
Albania investigated him, but the letters and actions of Fan Noli paint
a very different picture. Noli would declare to the Albanian people
after the triumph of the Partisans “…from the darkness of dead ends
we are entering the light and the open field of action with leaders
who have proven their mettle on the line of fire. These leaders must
be supported unconditionally, without reservations and without
saving, because they fully deserve the trust of the people. With these
leaders, the fate of the homeland is assured. Long live Albania” he
would also write” When Historians will look at the history of Albania
from the perspective of 100 years later, it would not be surprising for
them to consider the start of the real independent life of the
Albanian people not in 1912, but in 1944.”. Also to shed light
over the myth that Noli eventually supported Zog, i would like to
quote Kostandin Çekrezi, an Albanian patriot, historian and friend of
Noli, (to be exact Noli would describe him as a “statesman, who had
struggled all his life for his nation, and a great Albanian guy who had
to die poor”). So, Çekrezi mentions “one of the American officials
announced the news that a new government had been formed in
Albania under the presidency of Enver Hoxha (thinking the Përmet
Congress), the bishop’s face immediately the face of the Bishop
lightened and, as if he had the answer on the tip of his tongue, he
said without thinking at all: — Then, let’s give up Zogu to support the
new government. — No — said the American official — we will not
support it, because according to our information, it looks like a
pro-communist government, if not a communist one. — Then, the
bishop jumped — it doesn’t matter, those are our boys!”. As the war in
Albania went on Noli would write articles and deliver speeches to the Albanians of America and would end them with the phrases “Long
Live Albania” and “Long Live Enver Hoxha”, and when the last city of
Albania, Shkodra, was liberated, we would write in the newspaper
“Dielli”, “Albania is again under the magnificent flag of Skanderbeg.
This will be a holy day for the Albanian people. The role of General
Enver Hoxha is a triumph. Neither Enver Hoxha’s friends nor
enemies can dispute this truth”. This sentiment existed amongst the
Albanians in America at the time where they created the song:

“Enver Hoxha the young man,
People with love,
Charge him with responsibility
Guardian over all of Albania.
The world outside is hypocritical
They laughs ironically
Because their imperialist dreams
Find no place, only taunting”

After the liberation, Noli would send a letter to Hoxha to
congratulate him for the victory. In total 30 letters between Noli and
Hoxha are known but I am only going to quote a few words from
these letters because one doesn’t need more to understand the
attitude of Noli. In the first letter Noli would write “We feel proud of
you because you are the only military leader in this war who
succeeded in liberating the country from foreign occupation by his
own forces, without the help of any friendly liberation army; you are
also one of the few governments that can stand on its own two feet,
unsupported by friendly armies.” In the end of this letter, Noli would
wish the best and bless Hoxha and the other Cabinet members, and
Hoxha would respond positively and thank Noli for his kind words
and his immense support. The praises of Noli towards Hoxha had no
end, and he would mention “The situation of Albania today is muchmore favorable than it was in 1918… Especially this time, the
strongest guarantee for the future comes from the current
government of Enver Hoxha, who enjoys the trust of the people and
is capable to stand stands on its own two feet , when all the
governments of the liberated lands remain in power with the support
of the allied armies” and he would even declare that “Today
- I will announce with enthusiasm — that we have the best
government since the time of Skanderbeg” continuing the
comparisons with the National Hero Skanderbeg, he declared “ The
work that Skanderbeg could not complete was completed by the
people themselves, organizing the National Liberation Front… The
work that Skanderbeg started in the 15th century was completed by
Enver Hoxha in the XX century, with the superhuman sacrifices of
the people”. Noli became optimistic for the first time in his life for the
future of Albania and confident in the capabilities of the Albanian
state, in response to the war mongering declarations of the Greeks,
backed by the Western Imperialists, he would respond to them
“Today is neither 1912 nor 1920… Enver Hoxha has said more than
once that he is ready to fight for Albania. Enver Hoxha knows what
he is saying… The Greek people do not want war”, but Noli didn’t only
respond to the Greeks but also to the accusations of the Americans
against Albania, he would declare “This small country, these people
with a strong spirit are facing a series of opponents… There is no
doubt that America is intentionally or unintentionally becoming
a tool of England in the Balkans.”. Fan Noli made enormous efforts for
the recognition of new Albanian government not only with articles
and speeches, but by also contacting people inside the diplomatic
world that could help, like the important French diplomat Alexandre
Parodi, he even wrote a letter to Churchill, Roosevelt and Stalin but
of course all attempts were blocked by Anglo-American imperialism.
Noli’s praises wouldn’t stop to the figure of Hoxha but they wouldinclude the work and the acts of the new government that was “of
the people, for the people” according to him. In the newspaper
“Dielli” in 6/3/1946 we wrote “Under the leadership of Enver Hoxha
in May 1944 and January 1946 in Albania, revolutionary measures
were taken for the reconstruction of the country. The Agrarian
Reform took place, the class of beylers was finally overthrown. In this
period, Albania under Enver Hoxha proved to the world that
Albanians have an extraordinary ability to self- govern. This is proven
by facts, not rhetoric. Elsewhere in Europe, peoples suffer from
dreaded enemies: inflation and the black market. In Albania there is
neither one nor the other”. When the smearing campaign of the US
against the new Albanian government continued, Noli called it “a
campaign of shame”, and his words and actions against the United
States of America, would put him under the investigation of the FBI
for more than 10 year up until the point that due to his age he wasn’t
posing a real threat. In the wake of the support of the western
powers towards the beaten Ballists (National Front, collaborators of
Nazis) on March 26th 1947 he would publish the article “Two words
for the Ballists” where amongst others he said “There are no
Albanians so stupid as not to know that our ballistis died once and
for all, as soon as their politics went bankrupt. They collaborated
with the Italian fascists and the German Nazis in the hope that they
would win the war with them. With this tactic, they not only killed
themselves, but also almost killed Albania, which they promoted to
the world as pro-Italian and pro-German. As such, Albania would
have been torn apart without a doubt. God willing, the partisans with
Enver Hoxha at the head came out and not only took Albania out of
the abyss of death, but they also put it in the ranks of the living next
to the allied countries. The government of Enver Hoxha not only
saved Albania from death, but now lays the solid foundations of the
state with reforms and constructive works. These works can only bedone by a homogeneous, stable government, with a certain
progressive program” and “There is no Albanian in Albania or outside
Albania so stupid as not to understand these elementary things. So,
there is no Albanian with a dime in mind to support the ballists to
bring Albania to the brink of the grave for a second time. Then only
one hope remains for our ballistas. America creates a chaos, flattens

another chaos our ballists return to Tirana in triumph. This is the

They will do well to wake up, give up their dreams, and see the facts”
and he ends the articles with this conclusion “We come to the
conclusion: our ballistas fell, and have no hope of being resurrected
like vampires from some near reactionary war. So don’t listen to
them, but show them their place, and their place is in the graves of
fascism. On the other hand, Enver Hoxha’s government snatched
Albania from the claws of death with heroic sacrifices during the
war. Now it acts and triumphs with reforms and constructive work.
There is an excellent expectation that the period of peace will extend
at least fifteen or twenty years. So it deserves the unwavering
support of every good Albanian who wants the good of the
