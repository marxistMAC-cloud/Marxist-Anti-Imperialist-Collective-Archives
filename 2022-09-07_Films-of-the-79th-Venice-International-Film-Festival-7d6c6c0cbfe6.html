Films of the 79th Venice International Film Festival

The Whale

A reclusive English teacher suffering from severe obesity attempts to reconnect with his estranged teenage daughter for one last chance at redemption. The two grew apart after he abandoned his family for his gay lover, who later died.

Monica

Andrea Pallaoro finds a fresh angle on the trans experience. Monica is an intimate portrait of a trans woman who returns home after a long absence to confront the wounds of her past.

L’Immensità

Rome, 1970s. Clara and Felice have just moved into a new apartment. Their marriage is over. Only their children, on whom Clara pours all her desire for freedom, keep them together. Adriana, her eldest, has just turned 12. Adriana rejects her name and identity and wants to convince everyone that she is a boy.

Il signore delle formiche

Italian poet, playwright and director Aldo Braibanti is jailed in 1968 under a law criminalizing gay activity. He was sentenced to nine years’ prison, found guilty of plagio. That is, of having submitted another person, physically and psychologically, to his own will. In this case, a student and friend who was barely of age.

All the Beauty and the Bloodshed

The film examines the life and career of photographer and activist Nan Goldin. Her work often explores LGBT subcultures, moments of intimacy, the HIV/AIDS crisis, and the opioid epidemic.
