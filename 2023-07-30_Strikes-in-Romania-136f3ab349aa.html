Strikes in Romania

In the last two months, Romania has experienced strikes in healthcare, education and railway transport.

Healthcare workers have demanded implementation of previously agreed salary bills. Teachers demanded a 25% salary raise and setting salary for new entries to gross average salary. Railway transport workers demand better conditions.

Romania, like other Eastern European countries was destroyed by EU promises. People expected a better standard of living when they opted for capitalism. It made sense, given that the West had better living conditions than socialist countries. Unfortunately, socialist countries have never explained to their people that they will never have as good a standard as imperialists, because they don’t have compradors to exploit.

Additionally, Romania had a big austerity crisis in the 80s, because their leadership saw through the debt traps at one point, and decided to return all credits borrowed from the West, rather than to enter further negotiations with creditors. This led to general poverty, even though it was a good decision in the long run. The situation was not helped by the luxury lifestyle of the top of socialist bureaucracy, amidst extreme austerity.

Of course, the EU promises that Romanians will have Western living conditions, when they opt for capitalism and EU membership was not realized, and workers in Romania are not satisfied.

N.Stojanović 30/7/23
