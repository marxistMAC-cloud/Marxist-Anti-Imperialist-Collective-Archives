Major strikes in Finland

Suomen Ammattiliittojen Keskusjärjestö, SAK (A general union of Finnish labor unions.) together with STTK is currently holding a two-day political strike on the 1st and 2nd of February against serious curtailing of Finnish workers’ rights. The government is working on a collection of different cuts and reforms in the guise of balancing the budget, in reality many of these reforms have nothing to do with the national budget and are just a present for the bourgeoisie and foreign capital. Among the cuts and reforms are as detailed from the SAK website;

There will be no pay for the first day of sick leave

* “Relevant grounds” will alone suffice for dismissing an employee

* Workplaces with no shop steward will be allowed to agree local employment conditions that fall below the statutory standard

* Special grounds will only be required for temporary employment when the job lasts for longer than one year

* Reduced powers of the national conciliator will make it harder to settle industrial disputes

* A shorter notice period will be required for temporary layoffs

* Businesses with fewer than 50 employees will have no duty to re-engage redundant workers when operations recover

* Restriction of political strike action

* Restriction of sympathetic strike action

* A fine of EUR 200 for individual strikers when a strike is found to be illegal

* A dramatic increase in union strike finesA longer employment condition for earnings-related benefit

* The employment condition of eligibility for unemployment benefit will be based on prior earnings instead of working time

* Child supplements in unemployment benefit will be abolished

* The benefit portion that is protected in part-time working will be abolished

* Eligibility for unemployment benefit will begin only after phasing of outstanding holiday compensation

* Earnings-related benefit will already be reduced after two months of unemployment

* The waiting period for unemployment benefit will be prolonged

* Wage-subsidised employment will no longer count towards the employment condition for earnings-related benefit

* Benefits will be reduced for unemployed elderly workers

* Job alternation leave will be abolished

* Adult education benefit will be abolished

* The housing allowance portion that is protected during part-time working will be abolished and the allowance will be reduced

* Income support will be cut and made subject to tougher eligibility conditions

* The parental allowance increase payable for the first 16 working days will be abolished

* Following a budget session, the Government announced on 19 September 2023 that it is seeking alternative measures.

Evidently very sweeping changes. Many of these rights are the result of long and hard class-struggle decades ago, now the labor movement manages to pull off a two-day strike in a completely ineffective protest against removing all these rights in one swoop. Admittedly it is estimated that this strike will cost the Finnish GDP about 360 million euros, this is due to the strike affecting mostly proletarian i.e. productive fields. But when it is already agreed that the strike ends after two days, it amounts to essentially “we will comply with these changes, but we get to slap you in the face first” from the labor movement, barring subsequent strikes of course, none have been yet announced though. All this just highlights the complacency and degeneracy of the Finnish labor movement, which has grown fat and lazy over the time, taking for granted the good working conditions inherited from the previous generations, and subsequently upheld by the spoils of imperialism in the 2000s.

Now with the decline of the Western imperialist bloc, into which Finland had barely been included in just this millennia, Finland with its still mostly production-based economy is among the first to move firmly in the imperial- periphery. It goes without saying that with this comes the neoliberal shock therapy, with no imperialism to cover for the working conditions of yesteryear, severe cuts have to be made. And with no organized proletariat accustomed to class-struggle, they will be passed at breakneck speed with little resistance. The ongoing strike on the other hand is a good sign about the awakening of the Finnish proletariat to this reality, while it isn’t enough to halt the changes, it still shows that the labor movement isn’t completely subservient to the bourgeoisie, and perhaps will develop into the right direction as things worsen from now on.

Like said previously, Finland was added to the Western imperialist bloc quite late, really only starting after the dissolution of the Soviet Union, before that having had an economic incentive in domestic production and trade with the Soviet Union. In the 90s and early 2000s integration into the EU began, adopting the Euro among other things.

One can see the trend of importing over exporting (a staple of modern imperialism) started on the eve of the 2000s and went over the “threshold” in the 2010s, only to start trending towards export focused economy again, if still quite slowly. With this understanding of the Finnish economy, its development and relation to Western imperialism one can try to predict the future trend of the structure of the economy. I have predicted that the Finnish economy will enter a period of proletarianization, reindustrialization and imperialization as the West cannibalizes itself. Recent foreign investment into the resource industry, the decline of imports over exports, the accumulation of Finnish production under foreign capital and now this radical suppressing of workers’ rights seems to support this theory. The international bourgeoisie need new frontiers of resources and industries as they lose influence in the global South, and they certainly don’t want to deal with the good working conditions and high wages that exist in the West. It remains to be seen how far this is allowed to go before the Finnish nationalist movement grows big enough to stop it.
