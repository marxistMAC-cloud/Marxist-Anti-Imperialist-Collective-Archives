Bourgeois Scheming at the G20 Summit

The Group of Twenty (G20) Summit is an annual intergovernmental forum bringing together the leaders of the world’s most prominent states, most influential bourgeois organizations, and most vicious capitalist dynasties. This year, we saw the usual world leaders — Biden, Trudeau, Macron, Sunak, Meloni, Xi, Modi, Erdogan, der Leyen, and the rest — meet in Bali, Indonesia, to mingle with such fellows as Bill Gates, Klaus Schwab (of the World Economic Forum), Kristalina Georgiva (IMF), Tedros Ghebreyesus (WHO), Antonio Guterres (UN), and plenty more. Here, these fellows signed the “G20 Bali Leaders’ Declaration”. (1)

What are the contents of this Declaration?

The Bali Declaration seeks to address a number of “unparalleled multidimensional crises.” The first topic addressed is, naturally, the war between Russia and Ukraine. S.3 of the Declaration states that “most members” of the G20 “strongly condemn the war in Ukraine” and “deplore in the strongest terms the aggression by Russia and demand complete and unconditional withdrawal.” The G20 crew uses this as a basis to reaffirm their commitment to “defending the principles enshrined in the Charter of the UN.”

Meanwhile, the US and China made efforts to align on certain issues. Biden reaffirmed to reporters that the two countries came to certain agreements on the question of Ukraine, and assured that he “absolutely believes there need not be a new Cold War.” (2) Xi then went over to Canadian Prime Minister Trudeau and scolded him — as if he were a junior intern — for sharing conversations that were supposed to remain private. (3)

S.11 of the Bali Declaration addresses the “climate and energy crises,” with the G20 crowd making repeated appeals to the resolutions and research of the UNFCCC and IPCC. The Summit further saw the EU, Denmark, and Norway agreeing to spend a combined $20 billion for Indonesia to “decarbonize” its economy — that is to say, for Indonesia to de-industrialize itself at the command of the most-developed European economies. (4) Suffice to say, this “climate science” better be accurate. On that matter, we should here recommend Dr. Costella’s The Climategate Emails (2010) for a look into the internal bureaucracy of the IPCC.

Of course, pandemics were discussed as well. We have already heard Gates and other elite capitalists speak incessantly of a supposed “next pandemic” (as for what this “next pandemic” will be, look up some of the coronavirus strains and other deadly pathogens they’re playing with at Fort Detrick to get an idea). Just recently, the World Bank created a “Financial Intermediary Fund for Pandemic PPR,” which they colloquially refer to as the “Pandemic Fund,” while the World Trade Organization hosted a session on “Preparedness for Future Pandemics” at their most recent Ministerial Conference.

The WTO session and World Bank Pandemic Fund are both referenced in the Bali Declaration, in S.20 and S.22, respectively. The G20 countries “welcome the establishment of the new Pandemic Fund,” and “call on new donors” to join the project. Most revealingly, the Declaration states that the G20 countries “remain committed to enhancing global surveillance in order to detect pathogens.” Alas, they have finally tired of the “war on terror” excuse.

Another pandemic means another vaccine. Currently, we are seeing the whole narrative around the safety and efficacy of the COVID mRNA vaccines disintegrating before the public’s eyes. Just recently, Pfizer’s President of International Markets, Janine Small, admitted that the Pfizer mRNA vaccines were not even tested for blocking transmission of COVID. (5) New admissions regarding the vaccine’s dangerous side effects — effects we already knew about a year ago — are at last surfacing in mainstream news. And yet, despite these revelations, the Bali Declaration’s S.22 and S.23 reiterate that the G20 countries “recognize that extensive COVID immunization is a global public good,” “support the WHO’s mRNA Vaccine Technology Transfer,” and “welcome joint research and joint production of vaccines.”

And so we again arrive on the topic of the notorious “vaccine passports,” the likes of which weren’t able to be fully rolled out during the course of the previous pandemic. The bourgeoisie is still trying. S.23 of the Declaration states that the G20 countries aim “to facilitate seamless international travel” with “digital solutions and non-digital solutions, including proof of vaccinations.” Indonesia’s Minister of Health, Budi Sadikin, who attended the Summit, explained:

“Let’s have a digital health certificate acknowledged by the WHO: if you have been vaccinated or tested properly, then you can move around. G20 countries have agreed to this digital certificate using a WHO standard, and we will submit it into the next World Health Assembly in Geneva as a revision to international health regulation. “For the next pandemic, instead of stopping the movement of the people 100%, you can still provide some movement of the people. Hopefully for the next pandemic, we can still see some movement of the people, some movement of the goods, and movement of the economy,” Sadikin concluded at the B20 Summit.” (6)

The “next pandemic” this, the “next pandemic” that… And by “some movement of the people,” he means to say that the vaccinated, those who bend to the will of the global bourgeoisie and “drink the Kool-Aid,” will be allowed to retain their rights, while those who refuse will again be dehumanized and locked away in their homes.

Director-General Ghebreyesus of the WHO, who was in attendance to the G20 Summit, was probably quite pleased to hear these statements. So was Bill Gates, whose Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation is the single largest financial backer of the WHO, more than any government. By pushing these new security measures, S.23 of the Declaration says, the G20 countries can “build on the success of the existing standards and digital COVID-19 certificates.” Was the reader not warned that these “temporary” measures were never actually supposed to be temporary?

Also on the agenda is digital currency, which has recently been a high priority of the financial bourgeoisie. According to S.27 of the Declaration, the G20 countries “welcome the joint report by the BIS, IMF, and World Bank on options for access to Central Bank Digital Currencies for cross-border payments.” Truly, this is the highest stage of the abstraction of labor, where the real and tangible worth of human toil has been reduced entirely to an imaginary value on a computer screen. Consider the power of being able to delete a person’s life savings with the click of a button. Trudeau, whose government worked to shut down the bank accounts of anyone who donated to anti-lockdown protestors during the pandemic, is probably giddy with glee.

In case the reader does not think that digital currency will be used as a bludgeon for political power, we can simply address S.28 of the Bali Declaration, which states that the G20 countries “endorse the Financial Inclusion Framework on Harnessing Digitalization to Foster a Sustainable and Inclusive Economy.” S.41 assures that the G20 countries “remain committed to an inclusive approach that leads to greater social justice.”

What else is on the agenda? S.39 and S.40 asserts that the G20 countries “support the full inclusion of migrants, including migrant workers and refugees, in [economic] recovery efforts.” This means Great Replacement — if the reader doesn’t believe it, they can look up the UN Population Division’s 2001 report, Replacement Migration: Is it A Solution to Declining and Ageing Populations?

And of course, there is the usual obnoxious pandering to women. S.46 of the Declaration states that “women and girls continue are disproportionately affected by the COVID-19 pandemic and other crises,” and that for this reason, the G20 countries “reaffirm the commitment to put gender equality and women’s empowerment at the core of the G20’s efforts.”
